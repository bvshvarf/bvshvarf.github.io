Glossary

Nucleotide
A molecule that is composed of a sugar connected to a nitrogenous base (adenine, guanine, cytosine, uracil, thymine) and a phosphate group that make up the building blocks of both DNA and RNA.
DNA
DNA or deoxyribonucleic acid is a molecule composed of nucleotide pairs that resemble two backbones or strands of sugars and phosphates that are connected to each other by pairs of nitrogenous bases. These molecules store genetic information such as the instructions for making a protein or RNA molecule.
RNA
RNA or ribonucleic acid is a molecule that is typically composed of a single strand of sugars and phosphates, which are each connected to [a] nitrogenous base. RNA serves many roles including protein synthesis and regulating gene expression in living organisms, as well as storing genetic material for RNA viruses.
Biological Sequence
A continuous sequence of nucleotides or amino acids.
Galaxy
An open source, web-based platform for data intensive biomedical research.
FastQ File
A formatted text file with sequence data and their corresponding quality scores.

RNA-Sequencing
A sequencing technique that focuses on RNA molecules to provide information about gene expression and transcription.
ATAC-Sequencing
ATAC-Sequencing (or assay for transposase-accessible chromatin with sequencing) is a method for mapping chromatin accessibility (which is the level of physical access to the chromosomal DNA structure that is determined by its variable composition, structure, and dynamics). Through this mapping, ATAC-Sequencing can provide a picture of the regulation of a range of genomic processes such as DNA repair and replication.
WGBS-Sequencing
Whole Genome Bisulfite Sequencing is a sequencing technique that involves treating genomic DNA with sodium bisulfite prior to sequencing in order to map the locations of chemical modifications called methylation. This methylation and the locations at which it occurs play an important role in the regulation and expression of genome architecture and activity.
HIPAA
The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act is a federal law in the United States that regulates the handling of an individuals protected health information (PHI).
GDPR
The General Data Protection Regulation is an EU enacted regulation that deals with the use, collection, and storage of the personal identifiable information (PII) of EU citizens.
FDA
The United States Food and Drug administration.
